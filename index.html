Car Accidents in the USA

Is there a correlation between the circumstances of car accidents?

The map on the left is a representation of the number of car accidents per 10M people that occured in each state in March 2023. California had the highest amount of accidents per capita.
There are no discernable trends visible on this chart.
The map on the right shows the most commonly occuring weather condition during car accidents for each state. The three most common weather conditions were "Fair", "Cloudy", and "Light Precipitation".
This seems to suggest weather conditions are not a major impact on the occurences of car accidents.
The weather pie chart compares the amount of car accident occurences using the weather conditions at time of accident.
As mentioned previously, the three most common weather conditions were "Fair", "Cloudy", and "Light Precipitation", which reinforces the notion that weather conditions are not a major impact on the occurences of car accidents.
The temperature pie chart shows noticeably more car accidents for the temperature range of 41F to 70F.
However, it is important to note that the dataset consists of records from March 2023, which is spring in the US.
The hour of occurence chart shows that the amount of car accidents between 6am to 9am and 1pm to 6pm.
These are common travel times for those who work full-time jobs, which means the increase in accidents is only due to the increase in number of people travelling at the time.
Attempting to contrast both state and weather conditions with severity yields no new information, and no noticeable trends.
The severity bar chart shows that only accidents rated 2 and 4, on a scale of 1 (least severe) to 4 (most severe), occured in March 2023. There are significiantly more cases of severity level 2 than severity level 4.
The two stacked bar charts, which compare the state and weather to severity, only reinforce the sentiment that both geography and meteorology have limited impact on car accidents.
